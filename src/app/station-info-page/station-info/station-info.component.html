ʻIo was chosen as a name for the mauka station to honor the abundance of this sacred manu aliʻi (chiefly birds) that soars above Kohala. In moʻolelo such as that of Pikoi, the famous rat-snarer, the ʻio and the pueo appear as guardians of the uplands—echoing the mana and presence of these birds across the mauna lands of ʻIole. Their flight reminds us of observation, protection, and the intimate relationship between nature and our kūpuna.

ʻIole was chosen to honor the inoa of the ahupuaʻa and the ka'ao of Pikoi (legend of Pikoi), a wahi pana that carries the footsteps of aliʻi, kahu, leaders, plantation workers, and the generations shaped by Kohala Seminary and this ʻāina. To say the name ʻIole is to acknowledge all the people, stories, and lessons that have passed through this place and continue to give it life.

Together, ʻIo and ʻIole uplift the ʻike, heritage, and aloha of Kohala—grounding the work of the weather station in the spirit of the land and its storied history.
